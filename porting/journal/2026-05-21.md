## Document linearize-once RAD plan and comparative analysis

Commit date: 2026-05-21
Source commit: this commit (`Document linearize-once RAD plan and comparative analysis`)

Added two planning documents for the proposed migration of reverse-mode AD
toward a linearize-once plus transpose architecture:

- `porting/rad-linearize-once-transpose-plan-2026-05-21-en.md` is the
  implementation plan. It stages feed-forward RAD first, keeps
  `SigBlockReverseAD` as the temporal semantic carrier during the transition,
  separates Signal-level residual semantics from Signal-to-FIR scheduling, and
  analyzes delay, prefix, recursive, LTI/LTV, nonlinear, nested-recursion, and
  variable-delay cases.
- `porting/yolo-linearize-once-rad-analysis-2026-05-21-en.md` records an
  external comparative analysis of the same YOLo-inspired direction, including
  the stronger target-state idea of retiring AD-specific FIR machinery once a
  generic reverse-time region and cross-loop cache exist.

The plan documents the key storage distinction established during review:
primal-value tape/recomputation is driven by primal-dependent local Jacobians,
while temporal operators drive adjoint carries and reverse-time scheduling.
This keeps `NonlinearUse` as an abstract forward-value dependency instead of a
mandatory tape slot, and keeps tape allocation, recomputation policy, carry
allocation, loop placement, and backend-specific FIR details in
`signal_fir`.

External cross-checks were added for JAX linear transposition, Adept expression
templates, MIT 18.S096 AD notes, Rufflewind's reverse-mode AD tutorial,
time-dependent adjoint checkpointing references, BPTT truncation, and
reversible residual networks.

The JAX check was later tightened against the current source implementation:
`jax.vjp` routes through `ad.linearize(..., is_vjp=True)`, the default
`jax_use_direct_linearize` path runs `direct_linearize`, and
`backward_pass3` transposes the resulting tangent jaxpr with primitive
transpose rules. The plan now maps those pieces to `LinearizedProgram`,
`NonlinearUse`, and the future `LinNode` transposer, while keeping
Faust-specific temporal scheduling in `signal_fir`.
